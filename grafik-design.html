UA Hub Visual Identity

For this project, I developed a complete visual identity for UA Hub — a Ukrainian charitable association based in Ravensburg, Germany. The work included a full logo redesign, a defined brand color palette, and a new consistent visual style for their Instagram presence.

The redesigned logo combines elements of traditional Ukrainian embroidery with a heart symbol, reflecting both the organization's cultural roots and its charitable mission. The result is a cohesive and meaningful identity that communicates the values of the association at a glance.

Sclar Design Branding

This project was developed as part of a Graphic Design course at university, where I created a complete brand identity for my own fictional design studio — Sclar Design.

The name Sclar is derived from a Ukrainian word for a glassmaker, reflecting the studio's connection to craft and material. The logo design explores the dual nature of glass itself — combining sharp, geometric angles with smooth, organic curves to represent both its solid and liquid states. This contrast between hard and soft forms gives the brand a distinctive visual language that is both bold and refined.

Integration Project Identity

For this project, I developed a complete visual identity for a social integration initiative based in Ravensburg, Germany. The work included poster and flyer design, rollup banners, and a cohesive visual language for the event.

The central visual motif features two hands holding a colorful intertwining cord — a symbol of different people and different stories connected by a single thread: the shared experience of integrating into a new society. This imagery communicates the project's core message of unity, diversity, and human connection in a simple yet powerful way.
